# Sales Handoff Call — Relay Logic

**Date:** February 25, 2026
**Type:** Internal AE-to-Consultant handoff
**Speakers:**
- **Jordan Park** — Account Executive
- **Consultant** — Salesforce Consultant (you)

---

**Jordan:** Hey, thanks for jumping on. I wanted to give you the download on Relay Logic before you do the discovery call next week. Really excited about this one.

**Consultant:** Yeah, saw it come through. B2B SaaS, right? What do they do?

**Jordan:** Workflow automation platform. They sell to mid-market — mostly ops teams that are trying to connect their internal tools. Think Zapier but more enterprise-y. Growing fast, maybe 200 employees total, and the support org is feeling it.

**Consultant:** What's their support situation today?

**Jordan:** Honestly, it's pretty scrappy. They've been running everything through a shared Gmail inbox. Like, the whole team is just in one inbox replying to customers. It worked when they were small but they're starting to drop things. Customers are noticing.

**Consultant:** Ouch. How big is the support team?

**Jordan:** About half a dozen support folks, plus a couple of managers. Lisa Chen is the main person you'll be working with — she's their VP of Customer Success. Sharp, knows what she wants. She's the one who pushed for Salesforce internally.

**Consultant:** Good to know. What's driving the urgency?

**Jordan:** Two things. First, they've got tiered customers — silver, gold, platinum — and the VIP customers are not getting treated like VIPs. Lisa told me a story about a Platinum customer who had three open tickets and nobody noticed until they threatened to churn. She was livid.

**Consultant:** Yeah, that's a painful one.

**Jordan:** Right? So VIP handling is a big deal for Lisa. She wants smart routing — if a high-tier customer has multiple issues going on, that should get flagged and routed to someone senior. She mentioned different response times by tier too, but I don't have the specifics on that.

**Consultant:** Makes sense. What else?

**Jordan:** The other thing is just getting structure around their case data. Right now everything is just email threads — there's no way to tell what product a case is about, how it came in, any of that. They want to track product and channel on every case. Lisa was pretty emphatic about that.

**Consultant:** Got it. Any other wish-list items come up?

**Jordan:** Yeah, a couple of things in the parking lot. Lisa asked about a customer portal — somewhere customers could log in and see their tickets. And they use Stripe for billing, so she mentioned wanting to see subscription data in Salesforce at some point. I told her those are probably phase two, and she was fine with that.

**Consultant:** Smart to park those. What tier are we on?

**Jordan:** Service Cloud Enable. It covers everything they need for this first phase — the case management, the routing, the automation stuff. We scoped it that way intentionally.

**Consultant:** Perfect. Anything else I should know going into the discovery call?

**Jordan:** Lisa's going to bring her support team lead — I think his name is Marcus? He's the day-to-day ops person. Between Lisa and Marcus you'll get the full picture. Lisa thinks strategically, Marcus knows the ground-level pain.

Oh, and one more thing — Lisa specifically said she doesn't want this to feel like a big IT project. She wants her team using Salesforce within a couple weeks, not a couple months. So keep it practical.

**Consultant:** Good context. I'll keep the discovery focused and move fast. Thanks, Jordan.

**Jordan:** You got it. Let me know if you need anything — happy to do a warm intro if that helps. Good luck!
